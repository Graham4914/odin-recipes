Tangy Teriyaki Mirin Sauce

This sauce is so easy to make, but also easy to burn if you dont pay attention when cooking.

The finished product is a thick honey like viscosity with a great combination of sweet and salty taste!

Ingredients

  • 500ml soy sauce
  • 500ml mirin (japanese sweet wine)
  • 100ml rice wine vinigar
  • 5 teaspoons packed brown sugar
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • sliced red chili to taste

Steps

  1. chop garlic and ginger into small slices
  2. mix soy sauce, mirin, and rice wine vinigar into saucepan on bvery low heat
  3. geantly stir and after a few minutes add the sugar, garlic and chili
  4. keep a close eye on the sause as when it starts to reduce it can become thick very quickly and burn.
  5. when it starts to change consistancy and thicken up, take of the heat and let it cool down as it will thinken alot more once its reached room temperature
  6. poor into a sealed container and keep in the fidge ready to use on your favorite dishes!
